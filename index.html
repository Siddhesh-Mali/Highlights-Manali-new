Highlights Manali

Whether you are a nature lover, adventure enthusiast or just looking for a relaxing getaway, Manali has something for everyone.These are some of the highlights of Manali that you can explore on your next trip.

Rohtang Pass

Rohtang Pass

  • Rohtang Pass is a high mountain pass located in the Pir Panjal Range of the Himalayas. It is situated at an altitude of 3,978 meters above sea level and connects Manali to the Lahaul and Spiti valleys.
  • Reasons Why You Should Visit: Rohtang Pass is a popular destination among adventure seekers and nature lovers. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and glaciers. Visitors can indulge in activities like trekking, skiing, and snowmobiling.
  • Location-Specific Cultural Elements to Be Aware Of: Rohtang Pass is located in a remote area and has limited facilities. Visitors should be prepared for harsh weather conditions and should carry warm clothing and necessary equipment. The local people are known for their simple lifestyle and hospitality.
  • What to Eat In: There are no restaurants or food stalls at Rohtang Pass. Visitors can carry their own food and snacks or eat at the nearby villages. Local food options include rajma chawal, kadhi chawal, and aloo gobi.
River Beas

River Beas

  • Beas River is a major river in the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. It originates from the Beas Kund, located near the Rohtang Pass, and flows through the Kullu Valley before joining the Sutlej River in Punjab.
  • Reasons WhyYou Should Visit: Beas River is a popular destination among nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy activities like river rafting, kayaking, and fishing. The river offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Location-Specific Cultural Elements to Be Aware Of: The Beas River is considered sacred by the local people and is worshipped as a goddess. Visitors should respect the local culture and avoid polluting the river. The river is also an important source of irrigation and hydroelectric power.
  • What to Eat In: There are several restaurants and food stalls located near the Beas River. Visitors can enjoy local delicacies like trout fish, river-side barbecue, and thali meals. Don't forget to try the local honey and fruit juices.
Hadimba Temple

Hadimba Temple

  • Hadimba Temple is located in the town of Manali and is dedicated to Hidimba Devi, the wife of Bhima from the epic Mahabharata. The temple is situated amidst a forest of tall cedar trees and is built with wood and stone. It is a unique temple with an intricate architecture that showcases the skill and creativity of the craftsmen of the time. The temple's walls have intricate carvings depicting the tales of the Mahabharata. The temple's peaceful ambiance and beautiful surroundings make it a must-visit attraction in Manali.
  • Reasons WhyYou Should Visit: To explore the unique architecture and intricate carvings of the temple. To learn about the Hindu mythology associated with the temple. To experience the peaceful ambiance of the temple, surrounded by the beautiful cedar forest.
  • Location-Specific Cultural Elements to Be Aware Of: The temple is a sacred site for Hindus and is visited by devotees from all over India. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering the temple. The temple hosts an annual festival in May called "Dhungri Mela," which is celebrated to commemorate the birth anniversary of Hidimba Devi.
  • What to Eat In: The nearby market offers a range of delicious food options, including street food, local snacks, and traditional Himachali cuisine. Don't miss the opportunity to try "Sidu," a steamed bread stuffed with potatoes and served with spicy chutney.
Solang Valley

Solang Valley

  • Solang Valley is a picturesque valley located 14 km northwest of Manali. The valley is famous for its snow-capped mountains, lush greenery, and adventure sports activities. It is a popular destination for skiing, paragliding, and other adventure activities, making it an ideal place for adrenaline junkies. The valley also offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and is an excellent place to witness the sunrise and sunset.
  • Reasons WhyYou Should Visit: To enjoy adventure sports activities like skiing, paragliding, and zorbing. To witness the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and experience the tranquility of nature. To enjoy a range of other activities like trekking, horse riding, and camping.
  • Location-Specific Cultural Elements to Be Aware Of: Solang Valley is located in a remote area and is sparsely populated. Visitors are advised to carry all necessary items like food, water, warm clothes, and emergency medical kits. The valley is home to a few indigenous communities who have their unique culture and traditions. Visitors are expected to respect their customs and traditions.
  • What to Eat In: There are several food stalls and small restaurants in the valley that offer a range of food options like noodles, soup, momos, and other Tibetan delicacies. Don't miss the opportunity to try "Thukpa," a noodle soup with vegetables or meat that is a popular dish in the region.
